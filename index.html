A bit about me...

Formerly working in housing and estate regeneration, my interest in software engineering began when working as the lead user during CRM development and user testing at a housing association, Newlon Housing Trust. This was my opportunity to see how things worked behind the scenes for the first time and be part of improving the experience of the user. A short course with Code First: Girls and now the Software Engineering Immersive at General Assembly have taught me the skills which will enable a strong start to my career in software development. Qualities including being resourceful and thorough, and enjoying seeing the results of hard work, make me a dedicated and enthusiastic team member. I am looking for a role which combines my passion for continuous learning and skill development with the opportunity to work alongside diverse individuals with varying knowledge to achieve high quality results.

When I’m not at work, I can often be found reading a book in a corner, ahead of my next book club meeting. If I'm not there, I’ll have a pint of ale in my hand or cooking something up in the kitchen.
